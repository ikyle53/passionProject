Core Concept and Theme

Define the setting and narrative. Will it be sci-fi, fantasy, historical, or something unique?

Establish why players are fighting. Are there factions with distinct motivations?

Scale and Scope

What's the game's scale? 5-20 models per side? Large battles with 50+ models?

What's a typical game size? A board sized at 2' x 2'? 4' x 4'? What fits the narrative and objectives?

Consider playtime as well. 1-2 hours sounds like a good time

Core Mechanics

What would be a good resolution system: dice, cards, hybrid? How will actions be decided?

Define the turn structure: alternating activation, I go you go (IGOUGO), or something completely new?

Stats and attributes for units will need to determined. Simplicity wins but also needs flexibility for variety.

Faction and Unit Variety

Start with a few starting factions that have unique playstyles (eg., fast vs tough)

What are the unit roles (if any)? Leader, specialists, grunts. How will they differ mechanically?

Gotta make sure faction asymmetry feels balanced- each should have strengths and weaknesses.

Objective and Conditions

How will players win: kill points, objectives, or narrative goals?

Need to plan for varied missions to keep games replayable. This could come in the form of randomization via an app.

Can we balance competetive and casual play? Can we appeal to multiple motives?

Terrain and Environments

How will terrain impact gameplay? Will there be rules like cover, line of sight, or movement penalties?

Will terrain be fixed or flexible layout? Does it matter?

Will there be environment effects? Weather, traps, or other element like collapsing bridges.

Progression and Customization

Can players cusomize units (gear, skills, names, etc) or build rosters?

Is there a level up system to gain xp to gain new things/skills?

Keep in mind- Depth of the system may overwhelm some players, especially new players. Gotta have an attrative system with simplicity.

Accessibility and Components

I like the idea of digital rules

I also like the idea of digital list building

What physical components would be needed? (Dice, app, tokens, measuring tools)

Playtesting Framework

Whats the plan to test mechanics?

Should we include outsiders (community playtesting)?

How can we get good feedback?

Uniqueness

What is going to make our game stand out compared to every other game? Is it novel mechanics? A fresh setting? Good rules? No politically motivated bias?

I'd prefer to focus more on comunity than anything else. The game won't exist unless people exist to play it.
